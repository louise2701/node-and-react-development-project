Glossary

Welcome to the glossary page! Here, you will find definitions and example explanations for various technical terms covered in the lessons.

Mutation

Mutation refers to a genetic operator used in evolutionary algorithms to introduce random changes in individuals' genetic material, such as chromosomes or genes.

Example: In a genetic algorithm, mutation might involve randomly flipping a bit in a binary string representing an individual's chromosome.

Genetic

Genetic refers to the hereditary material or information carried by an organism's genes.

Example: Genetic factors can influence an individual's physical traits, such as eye color or height.

Crossover

Crossover, also known as recombination, is a genetic operator used in evolutionary algorithms to exchange genetic material between two parent individuals and produce offspring with combined traits.

Example: In a genetic algorithm, crossover might involve swapping segments of two parent chromosomes to create new offspring chromosomes.
